Distinguishable


This means that if you're using color to show something important, you need to also use another way to show it, like text or symbols.
Check for:
  • Instructions: Are there instructions that rely only on color (e.g., "Click the green button")?
  • Errors: Are errors indicated only by color (e.g., red text)?
  • Links: Are links only distinguished by color?
  • Alternatives: Are there other visual cues, like text labels, patterns, or underlining?

If your website has background music or sounds, people should be able to easily turn it off or adjust the volume.
Check for:
  • Controls: Are there clear controls to pause, stop, or adjust the volume of the audio?
  • Automatic audio: Does any audio start playing automatically?
  • Looping audio: Does any audio loop continuously?

This means the color of the text and the background should be different enough so people with low vision can read it.
Check for:
  • Use a contrast checker: Search for "WCAG contrast checker" online. Enter the text and background colors to see if they pass.
  • Text size: Smaller text needs more contrast.
  • Exceptions: Logos and inactive elements might be exempt.

People should be able to make the text bigger in their browser without the text getting cut off or overlapping.
Check for:
  • Zoom in: Use your browser's zoom feature (usually Ctrl + or Cmd +) to zoom to 200%.
  • Check for overlap: Make sure no text or other content overlaps or disappears.

Images of text can be harder to read for people with low vision or who use screen readers.
Check for:
  • Use text: If possible, use real text instead of images of text.
  • Exceptions: Logos and situations where a specific look is important might be okay.

When people make the text size bigger, they shouldn't have to scroll left and right to read the lines.
Check for:
  • Zoom and resize: Zoom in and make the browser window narrower. Can you read the lines without scrolling sideways?

This means things like buttons, icons, and graphics should have enough contrast with their background so people can see them easily.
Check for:
  • Contrast checker: Use a contrast checker tool to test the contrast of visual elements.
  • User interface components: Check buttons, icons, and other interface elements.
  • Graphics: Check important parts of graphics and diagrams.

This means there should be enough space between letters, words, and lines of text so it's easier to read.
Check for:
  • Spacing: Is there enough space between letters, words, and lines?
  • Overlapping: Does any text overlap when spacing is increased?

If something pops up when you hover over it with your mouse or tab to it with your keyboard, it should be easy to use and dismiss.
Check for:
  • Dismiss: Can you easily dismiss the extra content?
  • Time limits: Does it disappear too quickly?
  • Keyboard traps: Can you tab out of the extra content?
