Text Alternatives



This means that if you have anything that isn't text (like pictures, videos, or audio), you need to provide a text version so people who can't see or hear it can still understand it.

Check for:
  • Images: Do images have "alt text" that describes what the image shows? Are decorative images hidden? WCAG alt text decision tree
    • Decorative images should have an empty alt attribute, hiding them from AT
    • Important images should have alt text describing the image's purpose to the user with any relevant information
    • Right-click an image and look for "Inspect" or "View Page Source" to see the code, and look for "alt="
    • Open ANDI, select graphics/images and paginate through the items
  • Icons: Are icons explained with text or alt text?
  • Videos: Do videos have captions or transcripts?
  • Audio: Do audio files have transcripts?
  • Charts/Graphs: Are charts and graphs explained with text or a text summary?
